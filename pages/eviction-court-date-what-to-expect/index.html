Before the court date

  • Read all court paperwork carefully
  • Organize relevant documents
  • Arrive early if possible

During the hearing

Hearings are often brief. Both sides may speak. The judge may ask questions or issue instructions.

Why attendance matters

Not appearing can result in a default judgment. Showing up preserves your ability to participate.

After the hearing

Outcomes vary. Additional notices or steps may follow. Timing remains important after court.
