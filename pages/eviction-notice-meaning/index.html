What Does an Eviction Notice Mean?

Receiving an eviction notice can be stressful, confusing, and overwhelming. This page explains—plainly and calmly—what an eviction notice usually means, what it does not mean, and what tenants commonly do next.

An eviction notice is a warning, not an eviction

In most cases, an eviction notice is the first formal step in a longer process. It is a written notice from a landlord stating that a lease issue exists and that action may follow if the issue is not addressed.

What an eviction notice usually means

  • The landlord is claiming a problem under the lease or tenancy
  • A deadline is being stated
  • The issue may move to court if not resolved

What an eviction notice does NOT usually mean

  • You are immediately removed from your home
  • You must leave the same day
  • A court judgment already exists

Why timing matters

Eviction notices almost always include dates. Missing deadlines can reduce options later, so understanding your notice early is important.
