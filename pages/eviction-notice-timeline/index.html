Eviction Notice Timeline Explained

Eviction timelines vary by location, but many cases follow a similar sequence. This page explains the general timeline tenants often experience.

Stage 1: Notice is served

The process usually begins when a written notice is delivered. The notice often lists a reason and a deadline.

Stage 2: Notice period

  • Time to correct an issue (in some cases)
  • Time to prepare documents
  • Time to seek assistance

Stage 3: Court filing

If the issue is not resolved, the landlord may file a court case. Tenants may receive a summons or complaint.

Stage 4: Court date

A hearing may be scheduled. Missing this date can result in a default judgment.

Stage 5: Judgment and enforcement

Even after a judgment, additional steps usually occur before enforcement. Timing remains important.

Helpful: Understanding where you are in the timeline helps you decide what to do next.
